Human Resources: Making Goals to Reality

After years of ups and downs, I finally made it to where I wanted to be. Although it wasn't my plan A, it was in the direction I had planned for. I was offered the job as a Program Technician (PT) with the Behavioral Health's Human Resources division. Currenly, being a part of this team has made me look back at the last 5 years and realize the amount of dedication I put in. As a PT in human resources, I focus on 3 areas. This would consist of evaluations, timesheets, and terminations. Not only am I connecting with my direct supervisor, but I am interacting with deputy directors and the director herself along side all staff in the department.
The level of stress has not disipated entirely as there's a level of urgency that keeps my mind running. That urgency would be my plan A. You probably want to know what my plan A is right? Well, that plan A is why I am taking this course along side others. As a County employee, I found a field that I've always wanted to be a part of and that would be information technology. After countless years of research on a career path, the job application that caught my attention was working for Fresno County's Internal Services Department as a Information Technology Specialist.
