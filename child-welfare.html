Child Welfare: Welcome to The County

My first real position I considered was working as an office asstant (OA) for Fresno County's Child Welfare. It was the first step to something new. Although it was rough as well, I was proud that all my doubt was soon to change. The professional interactions with clients and supervisors was the experience I truly wanted. As an OA for Child Welfare, I got to learn great clerical skills that helped shape my soft skills. Other basic skills included data entry and understanding software applications. As I began my first 3 months there, my ambition to move up was lifted. I got to understand more about the County positions, and from there I set my hard plans to accomplish.
While keeping an eye on job postings, there were two positions within the County that I got to learn more about. My plan A and my plan B was set and my ambition was on fire. I knew that the my plan A would take some time, but I had to make the move on plan B when the opportunity came. 6 months after, I was given the opportunity to test for a Program Technician, and 3 months later I was able to say I got the job.
